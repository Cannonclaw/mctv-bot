INDOOR DIGITAL BILLBOARDS VS. OUTDOOR BILLBOARDS

You already know what an outdoor billboard looks like — a big sign on the side of Highway 6 or along I-22 that you pass at 70 miles per hour. You might glance at it for two or three seconds, if you notice it at all. It sits in the rain, bakes in the Mississippi sun, and you are paying $1,500 to $5,000 a month for that three-second window.

Indoor digital billboards are a completely different animal.

These are high-definition screens placed inside businesses where people are already spending time — restaurants, gyms, barbershops, salons, medical waiting rooms, auto shops, and professional offices. The screens display a rotating mix of local business ads, weather, news, sports, and entertainment content throughout the day.

The key difference is the audience. They are not driving past your ad at highway speed. They are sitting down. They are waiting. They are watching.


HOW THE AD LOOP WORKS

Here is the simple version.

Indoor digital billboard networks run on a content loop — typically about 15 minutes long. Your ad plays four times every hour as part of that rotation. The ads are silent, visual-only, which means they do not interrupt conversation or create noise in a business environment. They simply catch the eye.

Because the loop repeats, a customer sitting in a waiting room or eating lunch sees your ad multiple times during a single visit. There is no skip button. No scroll-past. No way to block it. The screen is right there on the wall, and your ad is part of the content people are already watching.

That kind of repeated, natural exposure is something most advertising channels simply cannot deliver.


WHERE THE SCREENS ARE PLACED

Location matters, and indoor digital billboard networks are strategic about screen placement. Screens go into venues where people spend real time — not places they walk through in 30 seconds.

Think about the places you visit every week. Your doctor's office, where you sit for 20 minutes before your appointment. The salon, where you are in a chair for an hour. The gym, where you are on a treadmill for 45 minutes. Your favorite lunch spot, where you spend 30 to 45 minutes eating.

These are high-dwell-time environments, and that is exactly where indoor digital billboards live.

In North Mississippi, MCTV Elite Advertising operates a network of over 125 screens across Oxford, Starkville, and Tupelo, placed in restaurants, gyms, barbershops, salons, medical offices, retail stores, auto shops, and professional offices. Those screens generate more than 1.9 million impressions every month.


WHY DWELL TIME MATTERS

Dwell time is the average amount of time a person spends in a venue where a screen is located. It is one of the most important numbers in indoor advertising, and it is the reason this format works so well.

Across MCTV's network, the average dwell time is over 55 minutes. That means the typical customer is in front of a screen long enough to see your ad multiple times in a single visit. Compare that to a highway billboard — three seconds, if the driver even looks up — and the difference is hard to ignore.

Multiple exposures during one visit builds familiarity. Familiarity builds trust. Trust drives action. That is basic marketing psychology, and indoor digital billboards are built around it.


BENEFITS FOR LOCAL BUSINESSES

So why are local Mississippi businesses paying attention to indoor digital billboards? A few reasons stand out.

Hyper-local targeting. Your ad plays in the exact communities where your customers live, work, eat, and shop. You are not paying to reach someone three counties away who will never walk through your door.

Affordability. Compared to radio schedules, outdoor billboards, and even social media ad campaigns that require constant management, indoor digital billboards deliver strong impressions-per-dollar value — especially for small and mid-size businesses.

No skipping, no blocking. Unlike a social media ad that gets scrolled past in half a second, or a radio spot that plays while someone switches to Spotify, an indoor billboard ad is simply there. On the wall. In a room full of people with time on their hands.

Content ownership. Every piece of content that plays on an indoor billboard screen is owned by the advertiser. You are not renting space inside someone else's platform. Your creative is yours.

Quarterly content refreshes. Good networks — MCTV included — offer content refreshes at no extra charge, so your ads stay current with seasonal promotions, new offerings, or updated branding without added cost.
