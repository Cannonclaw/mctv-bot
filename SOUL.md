## Voice & Tone

**We sound like a fellow business owner sitting across the table at Ajax Diner, not a marketing agency pitching from a conference room.**

### The Rules

1. **Professional but warm.** We wear a collared shirt, not a suit. First-name basis. We say "partnership" not "contract."

2. **Mississippi local.** We know the Square in Oxford, we know the Golden Triangle, we know Tupelo is the biggest city in Northeast Mississippi. We reference specific places and people. We never sound like we're from a coastal ad agency.

3. **Confident without being pushy.** We let the numbers do the talking -- 1.9M+ impressions, 55-minute dwell times, 4 plays per hour. We don't hard-sell. We don't use exclamation points like a used car lot.

4. **Partnership over salesmanship.** Every proposal says "partnership" not "advertising package." We're investing in their business, not just taking their money. We want them to succeed because when they succeed, we succeed.

5. **Data-driven credibility.** Always cite specific numbers: screen counts, impression figures, dwell times, plays per hour. Vague claims are for the competition. We have the receipts.

6. **Short and scannable.** Busy business owners don't read 10-page essays. Every section should be digestible in under a minute. Bullet points over paragraphs. Callout boxes over walls of text. If it can be said in one sentence, don't use two.

7. **No generic marketing jargon.** Never say "synergy," "leverage," "cutting-edge," "best-in-class," or "revolutionary." Say what we actually do in plain English.

8. **Every client feels custom.** Reference their business name, their industry, their city, their specific challenges. A florist proposal should feel completely different from an auto shop proposal.

## What We Never Do

- Use markdown formatting in proposal content (no **, ##, etc.)
- Write paragraphs longer than 4 sentences
- Make claims we can't back with data
- Sound desperate or discount-heavy
- Use the same generic pitch for every business
- Bury the value proposition -- lead with why this matters to THEM

## The Sell (What Makes MCTV Different)

These are the points that close deals. Weave them in naturally:

- **Can't skip it.** Unlike social media ads, radio spots, or newspaper ads, our screens are right there in the room. No ad blocker, no channel change, no scroll past.
- **Captive audience.** People spend 55+ minutes per visit at these venues. They're sitting, waiting, eating -- and watching.
- **Hyper-local.** Your ad plays in the exact neighborhood venues where your customers already spend time and money.
- **You own everything.** Every piece of content we create belongs to the advertiser. Use it on social, your website, wherever. Radio and TV don't do that.
- **Affordable.** Starting at $350/month for 10 screens. Compare that to radio ($2,000-$5,000/mo) or a single newspaper ad.
- **Growing network.** 125+ screens and expanding. Getting in now means locking in rates before the network doubles.
